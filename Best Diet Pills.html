Why Do Men Become Fat?

The basic cause of being overweight is simple—people consume more calories than they burn through physical activity. The extra calories are stored as body fat, which is an evolutionary development that provides humans with energy reserves for lean times.

But since food is now freely available—and most of these food items are packed with calories—there are no obvious circumstances that allow for burning off the excess fat.

In many places, men are more likely to be overweight. In the UK, this is true as well. Nearly half of all men in Britain are overweight, but this is true for just a third of the women. A quarter of all UK men are obese, but that only applies to 7% of the women.

25%of men in UK are OBESE

So what accounts for these startling differences? According to some experts, part of the reason for this is the social acceptance of overweight men. Since more of them are overweight, it seems like a normal situation for a man to be overweight.

So they are less likely than women to realize that their weight is unhealthy, and therefore less likely to do anything about it.

Of course, the main reasons for male obesity are still the usual suspects: they’re eating more and not exercising enough. Overeating packs in the calories, and inadequate exercise fails to burn through the level of calorie consumption.

So men end up with an energy imbalance that leads to stored body fat and excess weight.

But other factors may also be at work here:

    • Genetics. Just recently, US researchers have discovered a new gene found in human fat and muscle. This gene, Arrdc3, seems to cause weight gain in men as they grow older, but it doesn’t seem to affect women. In their study, the US scientists removed this gene in male mice and they no longer experienced age-related weight gain. Removing this gene from female mice didn’t have any significant difference at all.
    • Chemicals. One type of chemicals called phthalates is especially dangerous for men. These are the chemicals used in many household products and used as plastic softeners. According to the study published in the journal Environmental Health Perspectives, these chemicals can cause men to become overweight and to lower their sensitivity to insulin, which is a precondition for diabetes. These chemicals damage how men’s testicles function, and increased obesity is associated with lower testosterone levels in adult males.
    • Pollution. Another study conducted by researchers in Denmark found that men who have blood group O (which is the most common group) are more susceptible to becoming overweight when exposed to long-term industrial pollution at work, compared to men of other blood groups and all women regardless of their blood group.
    • Work environment. The modern work environment may also be a significant contributor to male obesity. It’s not only that men are spending more time at work, which leaves them little time for exercise. The act of sitting itself affects how men burn fat. Studies involving lab animals have indicated that muscles produce a protein that processes fat only when these muscles are flexed. When men are sitting down, however, their muscles are not tensed.
    • Corporate success. Women may complain about the difficulty of climbing the corporate ladder, but this comes with a cost for men. US studies have found that a male boss is 20% more like to be overweight than average men. In contrast, among female bosses the risk is only increased by 7%.
    • Lack of sleep. Losing sleep is a very common consequence of high pressure schedules. But it has a much more serious effect on the male metabolism. In Japan, researchers have discovered that when men sleep for less than 5 hours, it triggers a hormone in the blood that increases appetite. This is an effect not seen among women.
    • High calorie instincts. When people are stressed, they eat more. But men are more likely to choose high-calorie food items with or without stress. A study conducted by the American Society of Microbiology involving 14,000 adults demonstrated that men tend to go for meat and poultry. In contrast, women are more likely than men to choose fruits and vegetables.
    • Vanity. Men are also more likely to be overweight because they’re more averse to being seen as puny. While women may find thinness appealing for themselves, for many men this is a sign of weakness and lack of masculinity. Men don’t actually see themselves as fat—they consider themselves “well-built”. The ideal, of course, is to be big with muscles. But being fat is considered a better alternative to being skinny.
  • Weight contagion. Remember that saying about birds of the same feather? A man who has a fat friend doubles his chances of becoming fat himself. That’s because men compare themselves to each other, so having an overweight female friend doesn’t have the same effect on men. In addition, men are also more susceptible to peer pressure. Men get teased when they try to eat salads while others are munching on steaks.

Health Effects of Obesity for Men

Men’s health has taken greater damage from obesity, compared to women’s health. For example, since 2003, the cases of diabetes among men have risen by almost 33%. In contrast, for women it only rose by less than 25%.

That’s because men are more likely to put on the excess weight around the waist, where it circulates through the liver and cause metabolic problems like diabetes.

33%Increase in Diabetes under Men

Overweight men also have much higher rates for medical conditions such as stroke, heart disease, and cancer.

Studies have also indicated that failure to conceive may be more the fault of overweight men. According to an Australian study, obese men are 66% more likely to be infertile than men with healthy weight. The obese men are also 3 times more likely to have a non-viable pregnancy after going through assisted reproduction or fertility treatments.

The quality of the DNA in the sperm of obese men is poorer, and that the mitochondria (which power the cells) are not as active compared to the sperm of men with healthy weight. It has also been suggested that levels of metabolic waste products are higher in obese men, which can cause this problem in their sperm.

Other Problems for Men Caused by Obesity

Obesity may also be the cause of low self-esteem, and this is especially true among the younger set. For many students, overweight boys are seen as objects of ridicule.

They can’t play sports well, and girls reject them for boys with healthier physiques.

As they get older, the problem with disdain from others may continue. A study conducted by Yale University researchers discovered that weight discrimination has increased by 66% over the last 10 years. In the workplace, obese employees are regarded as sloppy, less competent, lazy, and undisciplined by supervisors and coworkers.

66% increase in weight discrimenation in workplace
